A quick comparison of Korean earthenware pots (뚝배기) for serving samgyetang, jjigae, and soups. Unlisted page for personal reference.

## What size do I need?

| Use case | Minimum capacity |
|---|---|
| Doenjang jjigae, sundubu (1 serving) | 500–700ml (17–24oz) |
| Gamjatang (individual bowl) | 1000–1200ml (34–40oz) |
| **Samgyetang (whole Cornish hen + broth)** | **1500–2600ml (50–88oz)** |
| Family stew (shared pot) | 2000–3000ml (68–100oz) |

For samgyetang: you need at least 1.5L. A Cornish hen is ~15cm across and you need broth around it.

## Recommendation

For samgyetang specifically: **go to H Mart and get the biggest one they have** (~$25–35 in store). You'll use it for everything — gamjatang, jjigae, sundubu. If you want to order online and don't mind spending, the BALWOO family-size is the safe bet.
