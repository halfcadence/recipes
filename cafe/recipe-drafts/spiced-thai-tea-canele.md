The grown-up version of the [Thai Tea Canelé](/r/thai-tea-canele-v2): the same strong steep of ChaTraMue mix, but with aged rum stirred back in and a chai-like warm-spice blend that leans into the tea's malty, faintly anise-adjacent base. A cool-weather, holiday-leaning rotating flavor — rum and spice over a caramelized shell.

## Ingredients

| | |
|---|---|
| 440g | milk |
| 28g | Thai tea mix (ChaTraMue "Number One" brand) |
| 38g | evaporated milk (or heavy cream) |
| 45g | butter |
| 1 | vanilla bean (or 8g vanilla extract) |
| 1.5g | ground cinnamon |
| 1g | ground cardamom |
| 0.5g | ground clove (or a pinch of nutmeg/ginger to taste) |
| 170g | granulated sugar |
| 2g | fleur de sel |
| 1 | whole egg (~50g) |
| 2 | egg yolks (~36g) |
| 40g | aged rum |
| 85g | AP flour |
| 15g | cornstarch |
| | Nonstick spray for molds |

## Steps

1. Heat the milk to 175°F — do not boil. Off heat, add the Thai tea mix **and the cinnamon, cardamom, and clove**, cover, and steep 12–15 minutes. Steep strong: the flavor mutes sharply over a 60-minute bake, so this is a deliberately heavy infusion (~7–8% mix-to-milk, well above drink strength).

2. Strain through a fine sieve lined with cheesecloth or a cloth tea filter — the mix is dust-fine; pass it through 2–3 times for a clean, deep-orange liquid (some fine spice will carry through, which is fine). **Weigh the strained milk and measure out 340g** — top up with fresh milk if short (the recipe starts with extra to cover absorption). Return the 340g to the pot with the evaporated milk, butter, and vanilla and warm gently to melt the butter.

3. In a bowl, whisk sugar, salt, eggs, and yolks together.

4. Slowly pour about ¼ of the warm infused milk into the egg mixture, whisking to temper. Sift in flour and cornstarch, whisk until smooth.

5. Gradually add the remaining milk, whisking until smooth. Stir in the rum.

6. Strain through a sieve. Refrigerate overnight (24 hours minimum) — the rum-and-spice character strengthens and rounds out over the rest.

7. Coat molds with nonstick spray. Stir rested batter gently. Preheat oven to 525°F. Fill each mold with 70g.

8. Bake at 525°F for 11 minutes, then drop to 375°F and bake 50–55 minutes more.

9. Unmold immediately onto a cooling rack. Cool 2 hours. Eat the same day.

## Notes

- **The rum + spice rationale.** ChaTraMue's blend is malty Assam, vanilla, and a faint sweet/anise-adjacent aromatic. Aged rum reinforces the vanilla/caramel backbone the way it does in a traditional canelé, and a restrained chai-style trio (cinnamon, cardamom, clove) leans into the warm, spice-forward reading of Thai tea that Western recipes lean on (star anise / cardamom). Keep the spice small — ~3g total — so it frames the tea rather than turning it into a generic chai. This is the "grown-up" SKU; the clean version stays the [Thai Tea Canelé](/r/thai-tea-canele-v2).
- **Cool-weather / holiday rotating flavor.** Warm spice + rum reads as fall/winter. Per [`../flavor-strategy.md`](../flavor-strategy.md), this fits the Lane A (seasonal holiday) / Lane B (tea) overlap — a chai-adjacent, spice-led rotating drop, not a permanent-core anchor. Run it as a weekly or daily-drop special in the cold months.
- **Cost note (WA spirits tax).** The rum is by far the priciest line on the sheet — Washington's spirits taxes (the ~20.5% spirits sales tax plus the per-liter spirits liter tax) make retail rum expensive, and it ran ~$1.83/batch in the historical costing. Dropping it is the single biggest cost cut, which is exactly why the [Thai Tea Canelé](/r/thai-tea-canele-v2) is alcohol-free and cheaper; this spiced version pays for the rum with its premium/seasonal positioning (limited flavors stay full price — no combo or sampler discount).
- **Alcohol / sellability.** The 40g rum (~16g ethanol) bakes down to roughly **0.27–0.33g ethanol per canelé** after a full ~60-minute bake — well under the **1% ABW** that WA's RCW 66.04.010 food exclusion uses to keep a confection out of "liquor," so it's sellable with no liquor license, provided the charge stays small and it bakes fully. List the rum in the ingredients (descending weight order) and consider a voluntary "baked with spirits" note. Full reasoning, swaps, and the lab-test-before-retail caveat are in [`../alcohol-in-pastry.md`](../alcohol-in-pastry.md).
- **Color** still mutes to orange-gold under the dark shell (the FD&C Yellow #6 dilutes in batter and dulls under browning); a few drops of orange gel dye are optional if you want it to read more vividly.
- Milk starts at 440g to cover what the larger mix charge soaks up; after steeping you weigh the strained milk back to a clean 340g, so absorption can't dilute the batter. The rum replaces the alcohol-free version's 40g milk gram-for-gram, so the liquid balance is unchanged.
- **Baking steel option:** with a preheated baking steel (tray on the steel), the Perfect-Loaf-style **450°F for 15 min → 350°F for 50–60 min** is a viable alternative to the 525→375 profile — see [`../baking-steel-notes.md`](../baking-steel-notes.md) and [`../experiment-start-temp.md`](../experiment-start-temp.md).
- Other technique is identical to the custardy [Canelé](/r/canele).
