# Flavored Canelé — Cost of Production per Unit

**Prices:** Fred Meyer / Kroger Seattle + Amazon, mid-2026, mid-range. Flavor add-ins from researched product picks (TJ's Pound Plus dark choc, Ghirardelli white wafers, Anthony's Dutch cocoa, Jade Leaf culinary matcha, Twinings Earl Grey, Davidson's Ceylon, ChaTraMue Thai tea, Carnation evap, Cointreau + E&J brandy at WA out-the-door spirits-tax rates). Ingredients + baking spray only — **contribution-cost**, consistent with [`canele-cost.md`](./canele-cost.md). No labor / rent / packaging. Low-confidence lines flagged `~?`.

Costing conventions: yolk charged per egg (1 egg = 18g yolk = $0.25 → $0.0139/g); whole egg by mass ($0.005/g); spirits g→ml at ~0.94 g/ml; steeped-milk recipes charge the **full 420g milk bought**, not the 340g measured back. Baking spray ~$0.03/mold.

| Chocolate Ferrandi (12) | **$0.53** | +$0.08 | $3.47 | $1.97 |

| Earl Grey (12) | **$0.64** | +$0.19 | $3.36 | $1.86 |

**Cheapest — HK Milk Tea (~$0.38):** no alcohol (the line that usually dominates), and its add-ins are cheap (evap milk $0.28, Ceylon $0.79). Skipping the optional vanilla bean drops it to ~$0.33.

**Most expensive — Earl Grey (~$0.64):** the only recipe with *two* premium add-ins stacked — bergamot Earl Grey leaves ($2.16) **and** Cointreau ($2.01), over half the batch — on a small 12-unit yield.

**Profit holds everywhere.** Even the priciest flavor clears ~$3.36/unit at $4 retail and ~$1.86 at $2.50 F&F. The flavor spread ($0.38–$0.64) moves contribution by only ~$0.26/unit — small against the price. This is **contribution margin** (ingredients + spray, no labor/rent/packaging), the same basis as the plain sheet; net business profit is far lower once the $21.30/hr wage floor, rent, and waste load in.

## Stocking note (tiny kitchen)

The flavor add-ins are mostly **shelf-stable and worth buying in bulk** — they spread their pack cost across many batches and free you from the perishable dairy/egg base.

| Product | Pick | Pack | Shelf life | Stock? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dark chocolate 70% | TJ's Pound Plus 72% | 500g bar, ~$6 | 12–18 mo cool/dark | ✅ bulk |
| White chocolate | Ghirardelli white wafers | 284g, ~$7 | 9–12 mo (heat/odor sensitive) | ⚠️ buy moderate |
| Dutch cocoa | Anthony's | 907g, ~$14 | 2–3 yr | ✅ bulk |
| Culinary matcha | Jade Leaf culinary | 100g, ~$22 | **1–2 mo once opened** | ⚠️ small pouches, fridge/freezer |
| Ceylon black tea | Davidson's / Frontier OP | 454g, ~$16–24 | 18–24 mo airtight | ✅ bulk |
| Earl Grey | Twinings loose | 100g tin, ~$10–14 | 18–24 mo airtight | ✅ bulk |
| Thai tea mix | ChaTraMue "Number One" | 400g, ~$14 | 12–18 mo | ✅ bulk |
| Evaporated milk | Carnation 12oz can | ~$2 | 12–18 mo unopened; **5 days opened** | ✅ size cans to batch |
| Orange liqueur | Cointreau (or triple sec) | 750ml, ~$47 WA | indefinite | ✅ compact, lasts |
| Brandy | E&J / Christian Bros VS | 750ml, ~$18 WA | indefinite | ✅ compact, lasts |

- **Matcha is the one fragile item** — it oxidizes (loses color/flavor) within ~1–2 months of opening. Buy 100g pouches, keep airtight in the fridge/freezer.
- **White chocolate** is more heat/light/odor-sensitive than dark — store cool, sealed.
- **Spirits carry WA tax once** (20.5% + $3.77/L, ~28–30% over shelf), but a bottle lasts dozens of batches, so the per-canelé hit is small; triple sec / dark rum are the cheaper substitutes.

High on dairy, sugar, chocolate, matcha, Thai tea, evap milk, and the spirits (WA tax math explicit). **Medium `~?`** on Earl Grey ($0.10–0.14/g), Ceylon ($0.035–0.053/g), Dutch cocoa, vanilla pods, fleur de sel, flour/cornstarch, and the spray — exact retail not pinned. Rankings are robust: anchored by the high-confidence dominant lines (spirits, chocolate, matcha). Figures are **ingredients + baking spray only** — contribution-cost, not net business profit.
