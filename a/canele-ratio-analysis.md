A comparison of popular canelé recipes by ratio, to understand what makes the interior custardy vs cakey and what drives richness.

## The Recipes

Six recipes normalized to percentage of total batter weight:

| | **Ours (Marchal)** | **Baken** | **Taste of Artisan** | **Saveur** | **Thermomix** | **Japanese** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Total batter | 1696g | 1030g | 1060g | 1060g | 860g | 860g |
| Flour % | **16.8%** | 14.6%* | 9.4% | 9.4% | 10.5% | 16.3% |
| Sugar % | 19.9% | 21.8% | 18.9% | 20.8% | 23.3% | 17.4% |
| Fat % | **9.7%** | 6.0% | 6.6% | 6.6% | 1.2% | 4.3% |
| Liquid % | 48.9% | 51.5% | 52.8% | 59.3% | 61.6% | 64.0% |
| Egg % | 8.8% | 7.8% | 14.2% | 9.4% | 8.1% | 11.6% |

*Baken's 14.6% includes 25g cornstarch which adds structure differently than flour.

## What Each Component Does

| Component | Role | More = | Less = |
|---|---|---|---|
| Flour | Structure, sets the interior | Cakey, firm | Custardy, soft (but fragile) |
| Sugar | Sweetness, caramelization, moisture retention | Darker shell, sweeter | Paler, less caramel |
| Fat (butter + cream + yolk fat) | Richness, mouthfeel, tenderness | Richer, more indulgent | Lean, less depth |
| Liquid (milk + cream + rum) | Hydration | Thinner batter, more custard-like | Thicker, more structured |
| Egg protein | Binding, setting, emulsification | More set, sturdier | Fragile, may collapse |

## Key Findings

**Flour is the biggest lever for custard vs cake texture.** Recipes that people describe as "custardy" tend to have flour at 9–14% of total batter. Above 15%, the interior starts to feel cakey or bread-like.

**Fat drives richness.** Our recipe at 9.7% fat is the richest of the bunch — cream + butter + egg yolks. The Japanese recipe at 4.3% fat was "custardy but not rich or deep in flavor." Fat = flavor.

**Egg yolks vs whole eggs matter.** Yolks contribute fat, emulsification, and custard richness without the structure that whites add. Recipes using mostly yolks tend to be richer and more custardy. Recipes using more whole eggs are sturdier (easier to unmold) but less luxurious.

**Cornstarch adds structure that makes things more "set."** Both Baken's recipe and the DIY cake flour hack (AP + cornstarch) introduce gelatinized starch that firms up the interior more than flour protein alone.

## The Custardy Sweet Spot

Based on this analysis, the ideal ratios for a rich, custardy canelé appear to be:

| Component | Target % |
|---|---|
| Flour | 12–14% |
| Sugar | 19–22% |
| Fat | 7–10% |
| Liquid | 49–55% |
| Egg | 8–10% (heavy on yolks) |

## Our Next Experiment

Reducing flour from 285g to 240g brings us from 16.8% → 14.1%, right at the top of the custardy range while keeping all the richness (cream, butter, yolks).
