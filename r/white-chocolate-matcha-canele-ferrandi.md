## Ingredients

| | |
|---|---|
| 400g | milk |
| 100g | white chocolate, finely chopped |
| 1 | vanilla bean (or 8g vanilla extract) |
| 160g | sugar |
| 2g | fleur de sel |
| 110g | whole egg (~2 large, lightly beaten) |
| 40g | egg yolk (~2 yolks) |
| 70g | AP flour |
| 12g | matcha powder |
| 20g | cornstarch |
| 60g | dark rum |
| | Beeswax for molds |

## Steps

1. In a pot, heat milk with vanilla to 150°F. Remove from heat, let cool 2–3 minutes.

2. Add chopped white chocolate, cover, let sit 3 minutes, then stir gently until melted and smooth. White chocolate is temperature-sensitive — be patient.

3. In a bowl, whisk sugar, salt, eggs, and yolks together until thick and slightly pale.

4. Sift flour, matcha, and cornstarch together. Fold into the egg mixture until smooth.

5. Pour the white chocolate milk into the batter, whisk to combine. Add rum, whisk again.

6. Strain through a sieve. Refrigerate 12 hours (overnight).

7. Coat molds with beeswax. Whisk rested batter until smooth (a skin may form — just mix it back in). Fill molds to 5mm below the rim.

8. Preheat oven to 450°F. Bake at 450°F for 10 minutes. Without opening the oven, drop to 375°F and continue baking for 50 minutes.

9. Unmold immediately onto a cooling rack. Cool before eating. Best the same day.

## Notes

- EXPERIMENTAL — adapted from the Ferrandi chocolate canelé by swapping dark chocolate for white and adding matcha. Untested.
- Flour increased to 70g (vs 50g in the dark chocolate Ferrandi) because white chocolate contributes zero cocoa solids — dark chocolate provides ~32g of structural solids for free that white chocolate doesn't. The extra flour compensates.
- White chocolate adds fat but no cocoa-solid structure — the flour (50g) and cornstarch (20g) carry all the structural load.
- Use good white chocolate (Valrhona Ivoire, Callebaut W2). Cheap white chocolate is mostly palm oil and sugar.
- Matcha at 12g should be assertive enough to cut through the white chocolate sweetness. If it tastes too sweet, bump matcha to 15g.
- The interior should be pale green and very creamy.
- Compared to the other white chocolate matcha canelé (#93, Marchal-based): this version has much less flour and should be significantly more custardy/pudding-like.
