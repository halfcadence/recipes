Cha yen from ChaTraMue mix, dialed back from the café default. The standard version is very sweet — a heavy pour of condensed milk plus added sugar. This brews the tea strong and sweetens lightly, so the malty, vanilla-forward tea actually shows.

## Ingredients

| | |
|---|---|
| 30g | Thai tea mix (ChaTraMue "Number One" brand) |
| 480g | water, just off the boil |
| 20g | granulated sugar (vs. ~40g+ standard) |
| 30g | evaporated milk |
| 15g | sweetened condensed milk (vs. ~40g+ standard) |
| | ice |

## Steps

1. Bring the water to a boil, add the Thai tea mix, and steep 5 minutes off the heat (or simmer 3–4 minutes for a stronger, more traditional brew). Brew it strong — the milk and ice both dilute it.

2. Strain through a fine sieve lined with cheesecloth or a cloth filter; the mix is dust-fine, so a plain strainer lets the fines through. Pass it twice for a clean, deep-orange tea.

3. While hot, stir in the granulated sugar and the sweetened condensed milk until dissolved. Taste — it should read pleasantly sweet but tea-forward, not dessert-sweet. Adjust by ~5g at a time.

4. Cool to room temperature (or refrigerate). Fill two glasses with ice, divide the tea between them.

5. Float the evaporated milk over the top — pour it slowly over the back of a spoon for the classic two-tone layer. Stir before drinking.

## Notes

- **Use real ChaTraMue mix** (the orange "Number One" bag), not plain black tea. The blend is Assam ("red tea") dust + sugar + vanilla-forward artificial flavor + FD&C Yellow #6 — the malty body, the vanilla note, and the signature orange all come from the mix. Note the mix already contains some sugar, so it is never fully unsweetened.
- **The sweetness lever is two ingredients: sugar + condensed milk.** The café default stacks both heavily (often 40g+ each per serving). This recipe roughly halves them — 20g sugar, 15g condensed — and leans on a stronger brew so the drink still tastes complete, just less cloying. For barely-sweet, drop the added sugar entirely and keep only the 15g condensed milk for body; for unsweetened-adjacent, use plain evaporated milk only and skip the condensed (it will taste markedly more astringent and tea-bitter — that's the trade).
- **Brew strong to survive dilution.** 30g mix to 480g water is a concentrated steep; the ice and milk knock it back. A weak brew plus less sugar tastes thin and watery, not "less sweet" — strength is what carries a low-sugar version.
- **The two dairies do different jobs.** Sweetened condensed milk adds sweetness + body + dairy-caramel; evaporated milk adds lighter creaminess and the floated top layer. Cutting sweetness means cutting the *condensed* milk specifically — keep the evaporated for the creamy finish and the photogenic layer.
- **Hot version:** skip the ice, serve the sweetened tea hot with the evaporated milk stirred in.
- Scales cleanly — it's a ratio: ~6–7% mix-to-water, then sweeten to taste. The [Thai Tea Canelé](/r/thai-tea-canele-v2) uses the same mix at a much stronger infusion (it has to survive a 60-minute bake).
