## Ingredients

| | |
|---|---|
| 2 oz | white port (or ruby/tawny) |
| 4 oz | tonic water |
| 1 slice | orange |
| 1 sprig | mint |
| | Ice |

## Steps

1. Fill a glass with ice.

2. Pour in port, top with tonic.

3. Add orange slice and mint sprig. Stir gently.

## Analysis

<div class="analysis" markdown="1">

Total drink: **~200g** (2 oz white port at ~19%, 4 oz tonic, over ice)

| Component | Amount | % of drink |
|---|---|---|
| Alcohol (ethanol) | ~9g | 4.5% |
| Sugar | ~13g | 6.5% |
| Water + dilution | ~178g | 89.0% |

**A low-ABV cooler — ~4.5% finished, and the sugar is mostly the tonic's, not the wine's.** White port is fortified but gentle; the 2:1 tonic pour dominates the glass and brings ~10g of its own sugar. A more complex, less boozy alternative to a G&T — ruby or tawny port pushes it sweeter and richer.

</div>

<div class="notes" markdown="1">
## Notes

- The ratio is 1:2 port to tonic. Scale to your glass size.
- White port is the classic choice — light and crisp. Ruby and young tawny work too for a sweeter, richer version.
- Don't use aged or vintage port — those are for sipping neat.
- A lower-alcohol alternative to gin and tonic with more complexity.
- Fever Tree or similar botanical tonic works best.

</div>
