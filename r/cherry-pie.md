Fresh cherries in a store-bought crust — the honest, easy version. The only fussy part is pitting; everything after is toss, fill, bake. Sour cherries make the classic bright pie; sweet Bing work with more acid and less sugar.

## Ingredients

| | |
|---|---|
| 2 | store-bought rolled pie crusts (bottom + top), kept cold |
| 900g | fresh cherries, pitted (~1kg / ~2.25 lb whole) |
| 130g | sugar (sweet cherries); 180g for sour |
| 32g | cornstarch (sweet cherries); 40g for sour |
| 15g | lemon juice (sweet cherries); omit or 5g for sour |
| 1g | almond extract (~¼ tsp) |
| 1.5g | salt |
| 15g | cold butter, cubed |
| 1 | egg, beaten with a splash of milk (wash) |
| | coarse sugar, for sprinkling |

## Steps

1. Pit the cherries. Toss with the sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, almond extract, and salt. Let macerate ~30 minutes so the juice releases and hydrates the starch.

2. Lift the cherries out with a slotted spoon into a bowl, leaving the pooled juice behind. Simmer the juice in a small pan over low heat 3–4 minutes until slightly thickened, then cool 5 minutes and stir it back into the cherries. This pre-thickens the free liquid so the filling sets reliably instead of running.

3. Put a rimmed baking sheet on the lowest oven rack and preheat to 425°F. Line a 9-inch pie plate with the bottom crust (optional: brush it with egg white to waterproof).

4. Add the cherries and their juice; dot with the butter. Top with the second crust or a lattice; crimp the edges and cut vents if using a full top. Brush with egg wash and sprinkle coarse sugar.

5. Bake on the preheated sheet at 425°F for 20 minutes. Tent the edges with foil once golden (~25–30 minutes in). Drop to 375°F and bake 35–45 minutes more, until the filling bubbles slowly in the **center** and the crust is deep golden — total ~55–65 minutes.

6. Cool completely, 3–4+ hours (overnight is better), before slicing. Do not cut it hot.

**A juice-set fruit filling, not a batter:** two-thirds of the filling is water and juice from the fruit, so the whole game is the ~4% structure — just enough cornstarch to gel all that liquid into a sliceable set. Sugar is high (~25%) because the cherries bring their own on top of what's added. There's no fat or protein network doing structural work; the set is starch alone, which is why the thickener amount and a full cool-down matter more than anything else.

## Notes

- **Sweet vs. sour.** Sour/Montmorency cherries give the vivid, classic cherry-pie flavor — use more sugar (~180g) and skip the lemon (they're already tart), and nudge the cornstarch up (~40g) since they throw more juice. Sweet Bing/dark sweet are milder and one-dimensional baked — use less sugar (~130g), add the lemon for brightness, and 32g cornstarch is enough.
- **Fresh cherries need real thickener.** They release far more juice than pre-gelled canned filling, so under-dosing gives soup. Cornstarch is the easy default; for a clearer, jewel-like filling use tapioca starch (1:1 by weight) or Instant ClearJel (~40–45g, no-cook and freeze-stable). Over-dosing turns it gluey — stay in range.
- **Frozen cherries work.** Use the same weight and **keep the juice** (don't drain it away — it's the flavor); add ~1 Tbsp (8g) extra cornstarch to cover the extra release, and you can toss them still-frozen.
- **The bubbling center is the doneness cue.** The middle heats last, so slow, thick bubbles in the very center prove the whole filling — not just the rim — has reached a full thickening boil and the starch has swelled. Pull it at edge-bubbling only and the middle never sets.
- **Cooling is the second half of thickening.** The starch gel firms up as the pie cools to room temperature; cut it hot and the still-liquid filling floods the plate. Give it hours.
- **Soggy bottom (thin store crust).** Stack the defenses: a preheated sheet pan on the lowest rack, the hot 425°F start, and enough thickener. Blind-baking isn't needed for an easy double-crust fruit pie; an optional egg-white brush on the raw bottom is a free upgrade.
- **Almond extract is the classic cherry partner** — but it's potent; ¼ tsp is plenty. Vanilla (1 tsp) works too, or use both.
- **Store-crust handling.** Let refrigerated rolled crusts sit ~15 minutes so they unroll without cracking; thaw frozen sheets per the package. A lattice or well-vented top lets the juicy filling steam off and concentrate.
- **In a cast-iron skillet (e.g. the [Field No. 8]({{ '/a/field-no-8-skillet' | relative_url }})).** A 10¼-inch skillet is bigger than a 9-inch plate — it holds roughly 1.5× the fruit — so scale the filling up to **~1150g pitted cherries** (buy ~3 lb / ~1.3kg whole), with **~165g sugar / 40g cornstarch** (sweet) or **~230g / 50g** (sour), 20g lemon, 20g butter. This matches real skillet cherry pies (e.g. FoodieCrush's 6 cups). Assemble in a room-temperature skillet — don't preheat the empty pan (you can't press dough into hot iron); keep the preheated sheet on the lowest rack for the bottom crust, then when you drop to 375°F **move the skillet up to the center rack** so the retained heat doesn't scorch the bottom, and **tent the edges earlier (~20 min)** since the metal rim browns the crimp fast. A store crust is a bit small to crimp fully up the sloped wall — re-roll it larger, crimp low on the wall, or use a lattice. Total bake runs a little longer (~65–75 min) for the deeper fill; still go by center-bubbling. You can't invert it — serve wedges straight from the skillet.
- **Cherries are acidic — a note for bare cast iron.** A well-seasoned skillet bakes cherry pie fine (it's a standard thing); the bottom crust keeps most of the filling off the metal. Just clean, dry, and re-oil the pan promptly after. If your seasoning is young or thin, either line the base with a parchment round first (also lets you lift the whole pie out) or use a pie plate for this one.
