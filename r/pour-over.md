## Ingredients

| | |
|---|---|
| 30g | coffee beans |
| 500g | water (205°F) |

## Grind

| | |
|---|---|
| Remi (conventional burrs) | ~2.75 revolutions from zero |
| Lagom Mini 2 (Mizen 48MS) | +15 dots from zero |

## Steps

1. Boil water. Let it cool to 205°F or wait 30 seconds off boil.

2. Grind 30g coffee. Rinse the V60 filter with hot water, discard rinse water.

3. Add grounds to the filter. Shake to level the bed.

4. Start timer. Pour 60g water in a spiral to saturate all grounds. This is the bloom.

5. Wait until 0:45. The bed should bubble and degas.

6. At 0:45, pour in slow concentric circles to 300g. Keep the stream thin and steady. Avoid pouring on the filter walls.

7. When the water level drops near the bed (~1:30), pour again to 500g.

8. Let it draw down completely. Target total time: 3:00–3:30.

<div class="notes" markdown="1">
## Notes

- The 1:16.7 ratio (30g/500g) is a good default. For a stronger cup, try 32g/500g. For lighter, 28g/500g.
- If drawdown finishes before 2:30, grind finer. If it stalls past 4:00, grind coarser.
- Water temperature matters more for light roasts. Go hotter (closer to boiling) for light, cooler (195–200°F) for dark.
- A gooseneck kettle is essential for pour control. A regular kettle makes this very difficult.
- Swirl the V60 gently after the last pour to flatten the bed. This helps even extraction.
</div>
