title: Matcha Shortbread
category: sweets

## Ingredients

| | |
|---|---|
| 75g | unsalted butter, softened |
| 30g | powdered sugar |
| 1g | salt |
| 13g | milk |
| 115g | cake flour |
| 8g | matcha powder |
| 20g | cornstarch |

## Steps

1. Beat softened butter with powdered sugar and salt until smooth and combined.

2. Add milk in two or three additions, mixing well after each.

3. Sift cake flour, matcha, and cornstarch together. Mix into the butter until it comes together into a dough. Do not overwork.

4. Wrap the dough in plastic and roll into a sheet about 12×15cm, roughly 1cm thick. Chill 3 hours.

5. Cut into batons about 1.5×6cm. Space on a parchment-lined sheet.

6. Bake at 266°F for 45–50 minutes until set but not colored. Cool completely before serving — they firm up as they cool.

<div class="notes" markdown="1">
## Notes

- A tender, sandy matcha shortbread. The low bake temperature keeps the green color bright — high heat dulls matcha to khaki.
- This is a shortbread, not a creamed drop cookie, so it holds its shape and does not spread. The cornstarch and lack of leavening make it sandy and tender rather than chewy.
- Use ceremonial or culinary-grade matcha you'd actually drink. Cheap matcha bakes bitter and brown.
- Want them thicker? Roll the sheet to 1.5cm instead of 1cm and add 5–8 minutes to the bake. Because there's no leavening, the baked thickness equals the rolled thickness — they won't puff or spread, so roll to whatever height you want.
- For a flavor swap, replace the 8g matcha with 8g hojicha powder, or 6g cocoa, or 5g instant espresso.
- Dough can be rolled, wrapped, and frozen up to a month. Slice from frozen, add a few minutes to the bake.
- Store in an airtight tin up to a week. They keep well — the low moisture content makes them shelf-stable.
</div>
