## Ingredients

| | |
|---|---|
| | **Rice** |
| 400g | short-grain rice, cooked and cooled slightly |
| 10g | sesame oil |
| 4g | salt |
| 5g | toasted sesame seeds |
| | **Fillings** |
| 4 | sheets gim (dried seaweed) |
| 3 | eggs, beaten |
| 1 | carrot, julienned |
| 150g | spinach |
| 4 | strips danmu (yellow pickled radish) |
| 100g | imitation crab or cooked bulgogi |
| | sesame oil, for brushing |

## Steps

1. Season the warm rice with sesame oil, salt, and sesame seeds. Toss gently and let cool to room temperature.

2. Make a thin omelet: cook the beaten eggs in a flat pan over low heat into a sheet, then slice into long strips.

3. Sauté the carrot with a pinch of salt until just softened, 2 minutes. Set aside.

4. Blanch the spinach 30 seconds, squeeze dry, and season with a little sesame oil and salt.

5. Lay a sheet of gim shiny-side down on a bamboo mat. Spread a thin, even layer of rice over the bottom two-thirds, leaving the top third bare.

6. Across the center of the rice, lay strips of egg, carrot, spinach, danmu, and crab.

7. Using the mat, roll up tightly from the bottom, tucking the fillings in, until you reach the bare seaweed. Seal the edge with a few grains of rice if needed.

8. Brush the roll with sesame oil. Slice into 1.5cm pieces with a sharp wet knife.

<div class="notes" markdown="1">
## Notes

- The rice should be just-warm and lightly seasoned, not sticky-sweet like sushi rice — kimbap uses sesame oil and salt instead of vinegar.
- Don't overfill. A thin rice layer and a modest line of fillings roll up far more cleanly than a fat one.
- Wipe the knife with a sesame-oiled paper towel between cuts for clean slices that don't drag.
- Fillings are flexible: also good with bulgogi, tuna-mayo, fried tofu, burdock (ueong), or fish cake. Keep a balance of color and a pickle for contrast.
- Roll tightly — air pockets make the rolls fall apart when sliced. The bamboo mat does the work; squeeze gently as you go.
- Best eaten the day it's made. Don't refrigerate if you can help it — cold hardens the rice. Store at cool room temp wrapped in foil for a few hours.
</div>
