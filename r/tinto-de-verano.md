## Ingredients

| | |
|---|---|
| 1 bottle | young, fruity red wine (garnacha or pinot noir), chilled |
| 720g | lemon or orange soda, chilled |
| 1 | orange, sliced |
| 1 | lemon, sliced |
| 180g | sweet vermouth (optional) |
| | Ice cubes |

## Steps

1. Pour red wine into a large pitcher.

2. Add the soda, leaving space for citrus.

3. Add lemon and orange slices and vermouth (if using). Stir gently to avoid losing carbonation.

4. Pour into ice-filled glasses and serve immediately.

## Analysis

<div class="analysis" markdown="1">

Total drink: **~1650g** (full pitcher: 750ml red wine, 720g lemon/orange soda, 180g sweet vermouth)

| Component | Amount | % of drink |
|---|---|---|
| Alcohol (ethanol) | ~99g | 6.0% |
| Sugar | ~100g | 6.1% |
| Water + dilution | ~1451g | 87.9% |

**A sessionable summer pitcher — ~6% ABV, about half the strength of the wine it starts from.** The soda roughly matches the wine by volume, halving the alcohol and supplying most of the ~6% sugar. Simpler and lighter than sangria (no fruit maceration, no brandy); the optional vermouth adds depth without much more booze. Per glass it's barely stronger than a shandy.

</div>

<div class="notes" markdown="1">
## Notes

- Use a young, fruity red wine that's drinkable on its own — nothing complex or expensive.
- If you can't find lemon Fanta, use a mix of lemonade and soda water.
- Vermouth is not traditional but adds depth. About 30g per glass.
- Don't prepare in advance — serve immediately to keep carbonation.

</div>
