## Ingredients

| | |
|---|---|
| 2 oz | bourbon |
| 3/4 oz | lemon juice, freshly squeezed |
| 1/2 oz | simple syrup |
| 1/2 oz | egg white (optional) |
| | Angostura bitters, for garnish |

## Steps

1. Add bourbon, lemon juice, simple syrup, and egg white (if using) to a shaker. Dry-shake for 30 seconds without ice.

2. Add ice and shake again for 15–20 seconds until well-chilled.

3. Strain into a rocks glass over fresh ice, or into a coupe without ice.

4. Garnish with 3–4 drops of Angostura bitters.

## Analysis

<div class="analysis" markdown="1">

Total drink: **~130g** (after shaking; 2 oz bourbon at 45%, ¾ oz lemon, ½ oz simple, egg white)

| Component | Amount | % of drink |
|---|---|---|
| Alcohol (ethanol) | ~21g | 16.4% |
| Sugar | ~8g | 5.8% |
| Water + dilution | ~101g | 77.8% |

**The textbook sour — ~16% ABV finished, balanced just off-dry at ~6%.** A classic 2:¾:½ spirit:sour:sweet ratio; shaking adds ~45g dilution and (with egg white) the silky foam. Bourbon's own sweetness rounds the lemon, which is why it needs less added sugar than a gin sour. Skip the egg for a cleaner, sharper drink.

</div>

<div class="notes" markdown="1">
## Notes

- The dry shake (no ice) aerates the egg white for a silky foam. Skip it if omitting the egg.
- Without egg white the drink is cleaner and more refreshing; with it, richer and more dessert-like.
- Bourbon is the easiest choice — its natural sweetness complements lemon well. Rye works too for a spicier version.

</div>
