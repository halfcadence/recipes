## Ingredients

| | |
|---|---|
| | **Cookies** |
| 185g | AP flour |
| 2g | baking soda |
| 9g | matcha |
| 13g | vanilla extract |
| 113g | unsalted butter, softened |
| 15g | peanut butter (or tahini) |
| 150g | granulated sugar |
| 50g | brown sugar |
| 3g | salt |
| 1 | large egg |
| | **Frosting** |
| 150g | granulated sugar |
| 24g | AP flour |
| | pinch of salt |
| 237g | whole milk |
| 227g | unsalted butter, softened |
| 4g | vanilla extract |

## Steps

1. Whisk together flour and baking soda in a medium bowl.

2. In a large bowl, stir the matcha and vanilla into a paste with a spatula. Add butter, peanut butter, both sugars, and salt. Beat until pale green and fluffy. Switch to a whisk and beat in the egg until smooth.

3. Fold the flour mixture into the wet ingredients until just combined. Refrigerate the dough, uncovered, while the oven heats.

4. Heat oven to 350°F. Line baking sheets with parchment.

5. Scoop 1½-tablespoon rounds and space them a couple inches apart. Bake until puffed and no longer wet-looking on top, about 15 minutes. Cool completely on the pan.

6. Make the frosting: in a medium saucepan off the heat, whisk together sugar, flour, and salt. Whisk in the milk until smooth. Cook over medium heat, whisking constantly, until it bubbles, about 4 minutes, then keep whisking as it boils until thick like pudding, about 2 minutes more.

7. Transfer the hot mixture to a stand mixer with the paddle. Beat on high until the bowl feels cool to the touch, up to 10 minutes.

8. With the mixer on medium-high, add the softened butter 2 tablespoons at a time, beating smooth before each addition. Add vanilla, then beat on high until very fluffy, 3–5 minutes.

9. Frost each cooled cookie. Serve immediately.

<div class="notes" markdown="1">
## Notes

- The frosting is an ermine (boiled-milk) buttercream — cooking the flour and milk into a pudding first, then whipping with butter. It's silky and not too sweet, which is what gives these the milky "latte" character. Don't skip the full cool-down before adding butter or it will split.
- The dab of peanut butter (or tahini) adds body and a subtle nuttiness without reading as peanut. It's the signature touch here.
- Use a matcha you'd actually drink — the flavor carries the whole cookie. Bump to 12g if you want it more assertive against the sweet frosting.
- Unfrosted cookies keep in an airtight container 2–3 days. Frost only what you'll eat that day; the buttercream is best fresh.
- The cookies bake up soft and cakey, not chewy — that's intended. Pull them while the tops just lose their wet sheen.
- For a matcha-on-matcha look, dust the frosted cookies with a little extra matcha or add sprinkles.
